"Thread.Sleep(0) might have an optimization to just call yield."So Thread.sleep(0) and Thread.yield() may call same system calls in some platforms.sleep(x) tells the JVM Thread Scheduler to actively put this thread to sleep and not run it again until at least x milliseconds have elapsed."Thread.sleep() will just pause the thread and not give away control."The semantics of Thread.yield and Thread.sleep(0) are undefined [JLS17.9]; the JVM is free to implement them as no-ops or treat them as scheduling hints.
